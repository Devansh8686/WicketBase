All 42 Laws of Cricket

Law No. Title Description
1The PlayersEach team shall have eleven players, one of whom shall be captain.
2The UmpiresThe umpires are responsible for enforcing the laws and ensuring fair play.
3The ScorersScorers must record all match events accurately.
4The BallDetails about the condition and specifications of the ball.
5The BatThe bat must be wooden and within size limits.
6The PitchThe pitch should be 22 yards long and well-prepared.
7The CreasesDefines bowling, popping, and return creases.
8The WicketsEach wicket has three stumps and two bails.
9Preparation and Maintenance of the Playing AreaThe field must be prepared to a standard for fair play.
10Covering the PitchThe pitch should be protected from weather when necessary.
11IntervalsRules for scheduled and unscheduled breaks in play.
12Start of Play; Cessation of PlayDefines when play starts or stops each day.
13InningsDetails how innings are structured and completed.
14The Follow-OnWhen the team batting second can be forced to bat again.
15Declaration and ForfeitureCaptains can declare or forfeit an innings strategically.
16The ResultHow the result of a match is decided.
17The OverEach over consists of 6 legal deliveries.
18Scoring RunsRuns are scored by batters running between wickets or hitting boundaries.
19BoundariesScoring 4 or 6 runs by hitting the ball to/over the boundary.
20Dead BallWhen the ball is no longer in play.
21No BallAn illegal delivery, e.g., overstepping the crease.
22Wide BallA ball out of the batter’s reach is called wide.
23Bye and Leg ByeScoring runs when the ball misses the bat.
24Fielder's Absence; SubstitutesRules on absent fielders and substitutes.
25Batter's Innings; RunnersRules for a batter’s turn and use of a runner.
26Practice on the FieldRegulations around on-field practice.
27The Wicket-KeeperSpecial rules for the wicket-keeper’s position and conduct.
28The FielderDefines legal fielding behavior.
29The Wicket is BrokenHow a wicket is legally broken for dismissals.
30Batter Out of His GroundDefines when a batter is out of their safe zone.
31AppealsFielding team must appeal for dismissals.
32BowledDismissal when the ball hits the stumps.
33CaughtDismissal by catching the ball on the full.
34Hit the Ball TwiceBatter cannot hit the ball twice intentionally.
35Hit WicketWhen a batter dislodges their own stumps.
36Leg Before Wicket (LBW)Dismissal when a ball hits the leg in line with the stumps.
37Obstructing the FieldIllegally hindering fielders is penalized.
38Run OutWhen a batter is out of ground as the stumps are broken.
39StumpedDismissal when a batter is out of ground and the keeper breaks the stumps.
40Timed OutBatter must be ready within 3 minutes of previous dismissal.
41Unfair PlayRules against actions like ball tampering, time-wasting.
42Players' ConductStandards of behavior and penalties for breaches.
